# Design Principles

The specification is governed by the following architectural invariants:

1. **Identity does not imply authority.**
2. **Key control does not imply accountability.**
3. **Capability does not imply permission.**
4. **Authority is contextual and bounded.**
5. **Delegation is distinct from ownership or control.**
6. **Downstream authority may narrow but must not expand.**
7. **Assurance applies only to the evaluated subject and scope.**
8. **Material change requires reassessment.**
9. **Revocation is incomplete until enforcement surfaces converge.**
10. **Consequential action must be reconstructable.**
11. **Registry decisions must be contestable and repairable.**
12. **Derived state must disclose provenance and freshness.**
13. **Technical interoperability does not imply governance recognition.**
14. **A registry supplies evidence and governed status, not universal trust.**
